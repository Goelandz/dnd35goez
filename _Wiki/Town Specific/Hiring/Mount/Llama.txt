Llamas are distant relatives of the camel. They are generally calm, trainable, and curious about new things. They stand about 4 feet tall at the shoulder and average around 350 pounds. Llamas make excellent pack animals and can also be trained to pull carts. Small riders also find them to be agreeable mounts. Llamas are statistically similar to camels, except that they are Medium-size, have 2 HD, and have a Strength score of 12.

Carrying Capacity: Light 129 lb., medium 258 lb., heavy 390 lb.; 
DC Young/Adult: 10/17; 
Young Price: 50 gp; 
Training Cost: 10 gp.
